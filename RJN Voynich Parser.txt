A  Recursive Structural Decipherment of Voynichese as a Hierarchical Functional Process-Operator Grammar
From primitive glyphs to macro-sequences, regime dispatch, and diagrammatic recipe systems

Ronny Juul Nielsen

Working draft for GitHub / Yale submission

May 2026

Draft status: This document separates the demonstrated structural parser from the functional 
role model and the historical recipe interpretation. 

Abstract

This paper presents a complete structural decipherment of Voynichese as a recursive 
hierarchical functional process-operator grammar. 
The system was not derived from a prior linguistic, botanical, cryptographic, or alchemical theory. 
It emerged after extensive computational testing of glyph behavior, token segmentation, positional constraints, macro
sequence structure, witness variation, regime behavior, and key-page alignment. 
More than one thousand scripts were used during the derivation process.

The resulting parser reads Voynichese at three structural levels: primitive glyphs, 
compositional tokens, and macro-sequences. A fourth interpretive layer, the regime dispatcher, 
is required because PROCESS, HERBAL, BALNEO, PHARMA, ASTRO, SPECIAL, ROSETTES, STAR, 
and ZODIAC pages organize the same operator inventory differently. 
The parser produces a stable token-by-token structural translation, while distinguishing structural meanings from 
historically precise substance names.

The central A-claim is that Voynichese is a recursive hierarchical process script. Glyphs form 
tokens, tokens form operator chains, operator chains form macro-sequences, and 
macro-sequences are interpreted through diagrammatic regimes. 

The B-claim is that the grammar yields stable process roles: transformation, transition, carrier, transfer, stabilization, 
extraction, output, residue/base, phase, closure, grade, and regulation. 

The C-claim is that the visual system is itself grammatical: roots, stems, leaves, flowers, stars, human figures, animal
like structures, teeth, claws, bones, scales, and heads function as diagrammatic classifiers 
rather than decorative anomalies. 

The D-claim is that these process roles fit historical medical, 
apothecary, and alchemical procedures such as maceration, filtration, fractionation, fixation, 
extraction, residue formation, and possible distillation or decoction.
The visual program is treated as a parallel diagrammatic grammar. Herbal plants are 
compressed vertical process diagrams; balneo scenes unfold hydraulic reservoir logic; pharma 
pages encode vessels and substance classes; astro and star pages use nodes, stars, labels, and 
radial structures as process categories. Key pages such as f49v and f66r are treated as operator 
demonstrations rather than ordinary prose.
The key-page evidence is summarized in Appendix E, where f49v, f66r, f69r, f75r, f75v, f76r, and f83v are treated as grammar-exposure environments rather than ordinary prose.

2. Methodological origin: no prior language theory

The investigation did not begin with a proposed language, cipher, botanical identification, or 
alchemical doctrine. 
The initial assumptions were minimal and material: the manuscript 
appears visually planned; the text is coordinated with drawings; the parchment was costly; the 
scribe was trained and confident; and the work therefore likely encoded a serious learned 
technical system.

The actual parser was derived after the initial computational phase. More than one thousand 
scripts tested glyph positions, token starts and endings, token decomposition, mini-token reuse, 
boundary roles, line roles, module discovery, recursive segmentation, grammar extraction, 
surface parsing, generation models, closure behavior, operation phases, entry transitions, and 
key-page signatures.

This matters because the model was not fitted to a pre-existing theory. The 
apothecary/alchemical interpretation became plausible only after the structural parser and 
process roles had already emerged.

3. Development pipeline
The derivation passed through a staged computational pipeline. The uploaded script log 
includes tests for boundary roles, line roles, module discovery, recursive segmentation, token 
decomposition, grammar extraction, surface parsing, generation models, closure-terminal 
behavior, operation phases, entry-transition matrices, and f49v margin-signature tests.

The consequence is a model that has both bottom-up statistical grounding and top-down 
grammatical coherence. It is not merely a set of visual associations.

The grammar is recursive. q-blocks can contain smaller q-blocks. Closure can occur locally and 
globally. This explains why Voynichese often looks repetitive while still behaving as structured 
process notation.
Q_CORE = Q_BLOCK+
Q_BLOCK = q + OPERATION + (Q_BLOCK?) + (CLOSURE?)
Therefore: q-blocks can contain sub-q-blocks
closure can be local or global macro-sequences can be nested




7. Primitive operator inventory
The primitive inventory must remain maximally consistent. The same sign should keep the 
same functional role across tokens, unless a higher macro-binding is explicitly active. In 
particular, d remains transformation/processing, a remains transition-to, and y remains 
closure/end. Compound forms such as daiin, dar, dol, and dam are therefore not independent 
lexical words; they are compositional target forms built from transformation plus a target.
The three initiation signs are kept distinct:
q = strong external initiation / process start / flow start
p = operational initiation / procedure-init / subprocedure-start often internal or embedded
f = material/preparatory/variant initiation often internal or embedded

This distinction is important. q is the strong active process motor, p is more procedural or 
subprocedural, and f marks a preparatory, material, or variant branch.

8. h-rule V6.44: activation, embedded forms, and remainder classes
The sign h means activation or execution. It must not be reduced to a mere component of ch, 
sh, cth, ckh, cph, or cfh. It can stand in four ways:
1. free primitive h
2. free macro with ch / sh
3. embedded c-form: cth / ckh / cph / cfh
4. rare non-c h-pair remainder: th / kh / ph / fh-rest


8.1 Free h
h is an independent activation operator.
Examples:
hy = h + y
   = activation finalized
oheol = o + h + e + ol
      = nodebase -> activation -> grade -> local carrier
qoiheey = q + o + i + h + ee + y
        = initiate nodebase/phase
          -> activation
          -> grade 2
          -> finalized
Therefore, h is not only something bound inside ch, sh, cth, ckh, cph, or cfh.


8.2 Primary free h-macros
ch = c + h
   = active coupled frame
sh = s + h
   = activated regulation/status


8.3 Embedded c-forms
cth = c + t + h
= active coupled transfer
ckh = c + k + h
= active coupled stabilization
cph = c + p + h
= active coupled procedure/separation
cfh = c + f + h
= active coupled variant/preparation


8.4 Sequential ch-forms
cht = ch + t
= active coupled frame -> transfer
chk = ch + k
= active coupled frame -> stabilization
chp = ch + p
= active coupled frame -> operation-init
chf = ch + f
= active coupled frame -> variant-init
Therefore:
cth != cht
ckh != chk
cph != chp
cfh != chf

12. Regime dispatcher
Token-splitting alone is not sufficient. The same operator inventory is reorganized by section, 
layout, and visual context. The parser therefore requires a regime dispatcher.
def interpret(parsed, section, visual_context=None):
if section == “PROCESS”: return interpret_process(parsed)
if section == “HERBAL”: return interpret_vertical_process_diagram(parsed, visual_context)
if section == “BALNEO”: return interpret_flow_reservoir(parsed, visual_context)
if section == “PHARMA”: return interpret_vessel_substance(parsed, visual_context)
if section == “ASTRO”: return interpret_radial_node_graph(parsed, visual_context)
if section == “SPECIAL”: return interpret_operator_demo(parsed)
if section == “ROSETTES”: return interpret_masterdiagram(parsed)
if section == “STAR”: return interpret_star_block(parsed)
if section == “ZODIAC”: return interpret_figure_zodiac(parsed, visual_context)

Thus, the same parsed token can be a process step in PROCESS mode, a vessel/fraction label in 
PHARMA mode, a node label in ASTRO mode, or a diagrammatic key in SPECIAL mode.

The parser does not simply decompose every form and declare success. It distinguishes multiple layers of validity.

Almost every form can be decomposed into primitive glyphs, but decomposition alone is not 
sufficient. A token may be glyph-legal while failing token, line, regime, witness, or visual coherence.

14. Witness variation and rescue classes
Witness variation is treated explicitly. The parser uses strict rescue classes rather than free 
correction. It may flag suspicious places, but it may not correct them merely to fit the model.

• FREE_H_PAIR_REMAINDER
• POSSIBLE_E_I_TRANSCRIPTION_AMBIGUITY
• OPEN_A_TERMINAL_FLAG
• MISSING_INITIAL_CLOSURE_GLYPH
• PARSER_GUIDED_VISUAL_CORRECTION
• WITNESS_REPLACED
• SHORT_LABEL
• H_STACK / repeated activation validation

Locked correction rule:
The parser may flag structurally suspicious places.
It may not correct them merely to fit the model.
Correction requires independent visual, witness, or local graphical confirmation.

16.3 Balneo key pages: f75v, f76r, and f83v
The balneo section contains more than one key-like structure, but they are not all the same 
kind of key. The correction is important:

f75v = preceding balneo demonstration / test sequence

f76r = actual balneo marginal letter-key column

f83v = visual fractionation demonstration, not the letter-column key

Thus f83v should not be described as the balneo letter-key column. It is instead a visual 
demonstration of carrier reservoirs, separation, liquid flow, and discrete particle output. 
The actual left-margin letter-label column belongs to f76r.


16.3.1 f75v: preceding balneo demonstration / test sequence

f75v behaves as a large matrix/distribution scene and also contains a short key-like 
demonstration sequence. It shows that balneo figures are not merely bathers, but process 
positions in a carrier system. The green field, repeated human nodes, and vessel-like enclosure 
make it a demonstration of how local process nodes are embedded in a shared 
carrier/reservoir.

The f75v key-like labels are not fully aligned with ordinary text lines; this is one reason to read 
them as a demonstration or test sequence rather than normal running text. The sequence is:
f75v.K:
s -> l -> l -> o -> r -> s?

The final s is not written as an ordinary aligned label in the same way. Grove noted that the 
final s of f75v.P1.5 may belong to the sequence. Under the present parser this matters, because 
the sequence then becomes a compact demonstration of regulated/status marking, local 
carrier, carrier repetition, process field, output/relation, and final regulation.
Working structural reading:

22. What is and is not claimed
The paper claims a structural decipherment, not a final conventional dictionary. It is legitimate 
to say that d functions as transformation and a functions as transition-to in the parser. It is less 
safe to claim that a specific historical scribe would have used precisely the modern word 
“transformation” or “transition”. The historical word may be “processing,” “bringing into,” 
“conversion,” “phase movement,” or another close equivalent. The semantic operator role is the 
claim.
Similarly, ol is not claimed to literally and always mean water; rather, ol-class is a local carrier 
or field. In BALNEO it may often correspond to pool, vessel, or aqueous carrier. In HERBAL it 
may correspond to tissue, substrate, or local carrier. ok/qok is not claimed to always mean salt; 
it is a fixed/stabilized phase, with salt-like product as a strong historical candidate in relevant 
profiles.




23. Falsification criteria

1. If q does not behave as an initial micro-operation marker in independent transcriptions, the model is weakened.

2. If y/dy/edy do not behave as closure/finalization forms, the closure model is weakened.

3. If ot/ok/ol/op families do not show distinct distributions, the o-field family model is weakened.

4. If f49v does not contain five y-closed vertical operator demonstrations, the key-page claim is weakened.

5. If f66r margin/label/main-text layers do not match operator layers, the SPECIAL/ASTRO key claim is weakened.

6. If Rosettes circles are not o-family heavy and channels are not routing-heavy, the masterdiagram claim is weakened.

7. If blue fields do not correlate with transfer/output families, the visual color role is weakened.

8. If green fields do not correlate with carrier families, the carrier visual rule is weakened.

9. If op/of/cph do not correlate with separation/output elements, the extraction role is weakened.

10. If randomized or shuffled token controls preserve the same behavior, the structural claim is weakened.

25. Conclusion

Voynichese is best explained as a recursive hierarchical process-operator grammar. The system 
is not merely token-compositional; it is sequence-compositional and regime-dispatched. Glyphs form tokens, tokens form operator chains, operator chains form macro-sequences, and macro-sequences are interpreted through visual and section-specific regimes.
The strongest conservative claim is that the structural grammar of Voynichese has been deciphered. 
The functional role model explains carriers, transfers, stabilizations, extractions, 
outputs, residues, phases, and closures. 
The visual classifier model explains why roots, stems, leaves, flowers, animals, people, stars, claws, teeth, scales, and chambers recur as diagram elements. 
The historical interpretation is that these roles correspond to technical medical, apothecary, or alchemical process notation.
If reproduced and not falsified, this model provides a coherent explanation for the text, images, 
key pages, section progression, cross-sectional motif recurrence, and the manuscript’s unusual 
visual logic.

This appendix reports a set of internal preliminary falsification-oriented tests performed on the H witness of the Voynich corpus. The purpose is not to replace the full reproducibility package, but to show that the main structural claims of the model produce the expected empirical behavior.

The tests are designed around the actual claims of the model. The model does not claim that Voynichese is a normal lexical language best modeled by raw word n-grams. It claims that Voynichese is a structural-functional operator script in which primitive glyphs carry semantic operator values, tokens are compact procedural instructions, and larger sequences are organized by macro-grammar and regime dispatch. The correct tests must therefore examine operator position, target formation, closure behavior, field-family formation, token compactness, and the difference between raw surface-token n-grams and functional role n-grams.
